# Value Based Demo Framework — Presales Enablement Session
**Audience:** Presales Engineers & Solution Consultants
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Format:** Facilitated walkthrough + live demo

---

## The Problem We're Solving (Before We Start)

Every room has two kinds of people in it: the person who needs to know *will this work*, and the person who needs to know *what will this do for me*. Most demos pick one and lose the other. This session is about how to hold both — at the same time, from the same URL.

---

## Agenda

### 1. Setting the Stage — Why This Framework Exists
**Time: 10 minutes**

- The classic demo failure: one audience, one lens, half the room disengages
- Business stakeholders don't care about APIs. IT won't trust a demo without them. Both are in the room.
- Introduce the **Value Based Demo Framework (VBDF)**:
  - Single entry point — one URL, no awkward pivot to a different environment
  - Two audience-aware paths that fork from the same source of truth
  - Built to handle the "can you show me the technical side?" question without breaking flow
- What you'll be able to do after this session:
  - Run the business track fluently for a VP/CHRO audience
  - Switch to the IT track without losing momentum
  - Read the room and know when to switch

### 2. Framework Orientation — The Navigation Guide
**Time: 5 minutes**

Walk through the `vbdf-demo-nav.png` reference slide together.

- **Unified Entry Page** — why a single URL matters in a demo setting (no "let me pull up a different tab")
- **Business Path** — KPIs & Value: what a business stakeholder actually wants to see

- Key insight: the data is the same, the story is different

---

### 3. Live Demo — Business Track
**Time: 15 minutes**

> **Set the scene:** You're in front of an HR VP, a CHRO, and an Operations Director. They've heard "AI-powered" a hundred times. They want to know what changes on Monday morning.

Walk the business path in sequence:

1. **Business Value Dashboard** — lead with the KPIs. These are the numbers they'll put in their exec report. Call out what's live, what's calculated, and what the AI surfaces automatically.
2. **360 Entity Profiles** — this is the "I used to have to open four systems for this" moment. Let it land.
3. **Process & Case Management** — workflow, SLAs, case timelines. Connect to real HR pain: compliance deadlines, escalation paths, nothing falling through the cracks.
4. **Compliance & Governance** — risk alerts and audit docs. This is the one that makes the General Counsel lean forward.

**Narration focus:** Outcomes, not features. *"This means you don't have to..."* not *"This does..."*

---

### 4. Live Demo — IT Track
**Time: 15 minutes**

> **Re-set the scene:** Same demo, same URL. Now the IT Architect and Integration Lead are driving. They'll kill the deal if they're not convinced it's real under the hood.

Walk the IT path in sequence:

1. **Integration Architecture** — show the MCP Server, API routing, and AI Agent wiring. This is where you earn credibility with the technical evaluator.
2. **API Endpoints & Operations** — live REST endpoints, GET/POST, JSON schemas. Let them see it's not mocked. If they want to try a call, let them.
3. **Connector Health Monitor** — real-time sync status across all systems. This answers the "what happens when something breaks?" question before they ask it.
4. **Data Flow & Error Handling** — reconciliation, exceptions, audit trail. IT wants to know who's responsible when it fails. Show them.

**Narration focus:** Reliability, observability, control. *"Your team owns this..."* and *"Here's what the audit trail looks like..."*

---

### 5. Context Switching in Practice
**Time: 10 minutes**

This is the skill that separates good presales from great presales.

- **The pivot moment:** what triggers a switch mid-demo?
  - A technical person starts asking technical questions mid-business-demo
  - A business leader tunes out during an API walkthrough
  - Someone new joins the call and changes the room dynamic
- **How to switch without breaking the narrative:**
  - Same URL — no new tab, no "let me just pull up..."
  - Anchor statement before switching: *"Let me show you what's actually powering all of that..."* (business → IT) or *"Here's what all of this means for the team using it every day..."* (IT → business)
  - Come back to where you were
- **Live flip exercise:** Demonstrate switching from the business dashboard directly into the integration architecture view and back — showing it's one coherent system, not two separate demos stitched together.
- **Discussion:** What objections does each path pre-empt? Which path do you open with?

### 6. Call to Action
**Time: 5 minutes**

Leave with a specific next step, not a general one.

**For your next opportunity:**
1. Identify who will be in the room — business, IT, or mixed
2. Choose your opening path based on the primary buyer, not the loudest voice
3. Prepare your pivot anchor statement for the switch
4. Run the demo end-to-end once before you're in front of a customer

## Facilitator Notes

- Keep the business demo conversational — avoid clicking through without narrating
- On the IT track, slow down on the API endpoints; technical evaluators will want to read the schema
- If the group is mostly senior SEs, spend more time on the pivot mechanics — they've seen the demo, they need the technique
- The "one URL" point is worth repeating at least three times; it sounds minor but it's operationally significant in a live customer setting
